DiningOut Optimizer — Architecture Comparison

What we've built, what comes next, and how DiningOut stacks up against every major restaurant-content publisher audited in Wave 0.
The one-line: Every AI schema tool on the market reads your page and classifies it. Ours reads your page and extracts the restaurants inside it.
Google confirmed our direction (May 7, 2026): Google's latest structured data update removed FAQ rich result dropdowns from SERPs and doubled down on entity-based schema — LocalBusiness, Restaurant, ItemList. That is exactly what DiningOut Optimizer builds. No pivot needed. FAQPage remains in our engine as an AI citation signal (correlates with higher AI Overviews citation rates in third-party studies) — we simply no longer promise a visual SERP dropdown from it.

What this chart says, in one breath: Today, the engine already does 9 of these 14 things — including 2 things no competitor in the restaurant-listicle space does at all (per-entry source-quote grounding, bulk archive backfill). v0.3 closes the 4 gaps that lift schema quality from F to B+/A- and adds 3 more capabilities nobody else has (stable @ids, HTML anchor parity, schema.org subtype dispatch). After v0.3, DiningOut owns the most machine-readable restaurant authority in every city it covers.
