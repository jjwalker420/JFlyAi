FIG.02 Untouched

Untouched.

Writers don't lift a finger. Editors get 20 minutes back per article.

30 minutes per article. Back in your team's hands. For backlinks and brand deals.

Writers own these.
  • Every narrative paragraph.
  • Voice, tone, opinions, recommendations.
  • The order of restaurants. The writer's editorial picks.
  • Photo selections and captions.
  • The intro and any storytelling.
Writers never see these.
  • JSON-LD schema in the page <head>. Invisible to readers.
  • A quick-facts block under each restaurant H2. Address. Hours. Price. Signature dish. Best for.
  • Table of contents at the top.
  • Short TL;DR paragraph. Answers the question for AI engines.
  • FAQ section at the bottom.
  • Better image alt text. Invisible to readers, critical for AI and accessibility.
Writers sign off on these.
  • H2 headlines only. "Safta's Israeli Brunch Buffet" → "Safta — Israeli Brunch Buffet in RiNo (Denver)". The writer's hook stays. Adds searchable keywords. The only writer-facing change. The only thing an editor approves.
Monday morning, after Phase 2 ships.
  • Writer hits publish in WordPress. Just like today.
  • Optimizer runs in the background. Extracts all 9 ranking signals in one pass.
  • Editor opens one screen with H2 rewrites side-by-side with originals.
  • Editor approves or edits each H2. About 90 seconds total.
  • Everything else auto-applies. The article never goes back to editorial.

Nine signals added. Zero words rewritten.

FIG.06 Roadmap

Roadmap.

After Phase 1 ships, here's where this goes. Five layers from ranking to citation to reservation.

  1. 1
    SEO

    Google indexes you. Classic ranking. The floor.

  2. 2
    AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

    Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity quote you in their answers. Structure, schema, and FAQ make that possible.

  3. 3
    AI Citation

    Models cite you by name. Entity-stable references hold across sessions. The source_quote field you saw in Proof is the mechanism.

  4. 4
    Entity Grounding

    Your restaurants become nodes the models know. Name, address, neighborhood, cuisine, signature dish. Stable across queries.

  5. 5
    Reservation + affiliate surface

    Mediavine display ads. Affiliate reservation links. Brand deals. All three already monetize your traffic. Layers 1 through 4 turn DiningOut into the source they pay for.

Each layer unlocks the one above it. Layer 5 is the payoff.

V2: editors own the in-place edits
  • Editors edit each field in WordPress. Fast-facts, FAQ, alt text, H2s. All in place.
  • Less back-and-forth on tuning rounds.
  • Builds on the Phase 2 plugin. No new architecture.
  • Timeline: Phase 2.5. Four to six weeks after Phase 2 ships.
V3: writers drop in, the draft comes back ready
  • Writers drop articles into a shared folder or Google Doc.
  • The system handles classification, extraction, optimization, image alt, and schema.
  • A Slack note lands. Your draft is ready in WordPress. Review or publish.
  • Timeline: post Phase 3. Needs Phase 2 stability and writer training.

A Monday in 2027. Linnea finishes a piece in Google Docs and drops it in the team folder. An hour later, a Slack note lands. Linnea's chefs piece is ready in WordPress. Review or publish. The editor opens the draft. Fast-facts in place. FAQ grounded. H2s ready for approval. Schema validates clean. Two minutes to read. Ninety seconds to approve. One click to publish. The 30 minutes per article you named on the call. Gone.
