# Copy and Paste Prompts

These are ready to use prompts, Mark, one for every kind of document you sell. They take the blank screen out of the equation. You never have to figure out what to say. You paste one in, fill in a couple of blanks, and start talking.

## How to use these

1. Open the Claude desktop app.
2. Pick the prompt below for the document you want to build.
3. Copy it and paste it into Claude.
4. Replace anything in [square brackets] with the real details. For example, change [concept name] to Tap and Burger, or to the real name.
5. Send it. Then just talk, the way you would explain the job to a sharp new manager. Claude will ask questions if you skip something.
6. When you are done talking, say "okay, write the full document now."
7. Read the draft. Fix anything wrong by talking again. Save it when it sounds like you.

A few tips that make every prompt work better.

- Talk like a person, not a writer. Explain it out loud the way you actually would.
- If Claude makes it too fancy or too corporate, say "make it simpler and warmer, plain words." It will.
- If it gets a detail wrong, just say the right version. It fixes it instantly.
- Always end by saving two copies. One in your Finished folder for the client, and one in your Templates folder with the names removed so you can sell it again.

## The master prompt, works for anything

If you ever want to build something not listed below, use this and fill in the blanks.

```
You are helping me, a hospitality operator with 25 years of experience, write a
[type of document] for a [concept type, for example casual burger and beer spot]
called [concept name].

The reader is [who will use it, for example a brand new server].
The goal of the document is [what it should accomplish].
Keep the tone warm, clear, and plain. Short sentences. No jargon.

I am going to talk you through how this really works, from my own experience.
Take notes and ask me questions if I skip something important. Do not write the
document yet. When I say I am done, write it up as a clean, organized, ready to
use document with clear headings and steps.
```

## Operations core

### Opening and closing procedures
```
Help me write the opening and closing procedures for [concept name], a
[concept type]. I want one document that covers front of house and back of house,
step by step, so any team member could open or close the building correctly.
I am going to walk you through how we really open and close. Ask questions if I
miss a station or a step. When I say done, write it as a clean checklist style
procedure with sections and checkboxes. Keep it plain and easy to follow.
```

### Cash handling and end of night reconciliation
```
Help me write a cash handling procedure for [concept name]. It should cover
setting the drawer, handling cash during service, comps and voids, and counting
down and reconciling at the end of the night. The reader is a shift manager.
I will talk you through how we do it. Ask about anything I skip. Then write it
as a clear step by step procedure, plain language, with the why behind each
control so people follow it.
```

### Cleaning and sanitation schedule
```
Help me write a cleaning and sanitation schedule for [concept name]. I want
daily, weekly, and monthly tasks for both front of house and back of house, with
who is responsible and how to do each one right. I will talk you through it.
Ask questions if I miss an area or a piece of equipment. Then write it as a clean
schedule with checkboxes and sign off lines.
```

### Inventory, ordering, and par levels
```
Help me write an inventory and ordering procedure for [concept name]. It should
cover taking inventory, setting par levels, placing orders, and receiving
deliveries correctly. The reader is a manager or a lead. I will walk you through
how we really do it. Ask about anything I skip. Then write it as a clear step by
step procedure with any forms or simple tables that would help.
```

### Food safety and health code procedures
```
Help me write the food safety procedures for [concept name]. Cover temperature
logs, safe storage and first in first out, cross contamination, hand washing,
cleaning and sanitizing, and what to do for a health inspection. The reader is
the whole team. I will talk you through our standards. Ask questions if I miss
something. Then write it as a clear, plain procedure people will actually follow.
```

### Emergency and incident procedures
```
Help me write emergency and incident procedures for [concept name]. Cover a
medical emergency, a fire, a security or violent situation, a guest who has had
too much to drink, refusing service, and a serious guest complaint. The reader
is any team member or manager. I will talk you through how we handle each one.
Ask questions if I miss a scenario. Then write it as clear, calm, step by step
guidance, with who to call and what to document.
```

### Manager daily checklist and shift handover
```
Help me write a manager's daily operations checklist and shift handover for
[concept name]. It should cover what a manager checks at open, during service,
and at close, and what they hand off to the next manager. I will walk you through
how a good manager runs a shift here. Ask questions if I skip something. Then
write it as a clean checklist with a shift handover section.
```

## Employee and training

### New hire onboarding program
```
Help me write a new hire onboarding program for [concept name], a [concept type].
It should cover the first day, the first week, and the first 30 days, including
paperwork handoff to the right person, training schedule, and what success looks
like. Keep my lane to operations and training, not HR compliance. I will talk you
through it. Ask questions if I skip a step. Then write it as a clear, welcoming
onboarding guide.
```

### Server training manual
```
Help me write a complete server training manual for [concept name], a
[concept type]. Cover our standards, appearance, opening setup, knowing the floor,
the full steps of service from greet to farewell, menu and drink knowledge,
responsible alcohol service, handling complaints, the point of sale, and closing
side work. End with a training schedule and a sign off page. I will talk you
through how a server really learns this job. Ask questions if I skip something.
Then write it as a clean, warm manual a brand new server could follow.
```

### Bartender training manual
```
Help me write a complete bartender training manual for [concept name]. Cover bar
setup and breakdown, our pour standards, the cocktail and beer list, speed and
station organization, responsible service and checking ID, cash and tabs, guest
interaction, and cleaning and sanitation. End with a training schedule and a sign
off page. I will talk you through how we run our bar. Ask questions if I skip
something. Then write it as a clean, practical manual.
```

### Host and door training
```
Help me write a host training guide for [concept name]. Cover greeting guests,
managing the wait and the seating chart, working with servers on table flow,
phone and reservations, to go handoff, and reading the room. I will talk you
through it. Ask questions if I skip something. Then write it as a clear, warm
guide a new host could follow on day one.
```

### Steps of service guide
```
Help me write a one page steps of service guide for [concept name] that any
front of house team member can keep in their apron. I will talk you through every
step from greet to farewell with our timing standards. Ask questions if I skip a
step. Then write it as a tight, scannable quick reference.
```

### Menu and bar knowledge guide
```
Help me write a menu and bar knowledge guide for [concept name] that helps the
team describe and recommend our food and drinks honestly. I will talk you through
our signature items, what makes each special, common allergens, and what pairs
well. Ask questions if I skip something. Then write it as a clear guide with a
short mouthwatering description for each key item.
```

### Coaching and performance conversation framework
```
Help me write a simple coaching framework for managers at [concept name], for
giving feedback, running a quick correction conversation, and recognizing great
work. Keep it to operations and floor performance, not formal HR discipline.
I will talk you through how a good manager coaches here. Ask questions if I skip
something. Then write it as a short, practical guide with example phrases.
```

## Standards and scaling

### Brand and service standards guide
```
Help me write a brand and service standards guide for [group or concept name].
This defines what consistent looks like across every location: the feel, the
service standards, the non negotiables, and the little things that make us us.
I will talk you through what makes our experience consistent. Ask questions if I
skip something. Then write it as a clear standards document the whole group can
align to.
```

### Audit and self inspection checklist
```
Help me write a self inspection and audit checklist for [concept name] so a
manager can walk the building and score whether we are meeting our standards on
cleanliness, service, food safety, and the guest experience. I will talk you
through what to check. Ask questions if I skip an area. Then write it as a
scored checklist with pass or fail and notes.
```

### New location opening playbook, your flagship
```
Help me write a new location opening playbook for [group name], a repeatable plan
for opening a new [concept type] location. Cover a countdown timeline from lease
signing to opening day, the build and equipment checklist, hiring and the
training calendar, ordering and vendor setup, pre opening punch lists, the health
and licensing steps at a high level, the soft opening plan, and the first week
operating plan. This is my most valuable product, so make it thorough and
genuinely useful. I will talk you through how a great opening really runs, from
my own experience opening many concepts. Ask me questions if I skip a phase or a
task. Build it section by section. When I say done, write it as a complete,
organized playbook with timelines and checklists.
```

### Recipe and plating spec template
```
Help me build a recipe and plating spec template for [concept name], so every
location makes every dish the same way. Cover ingredients and amounts, build
steps, plating, portion and cost notes, and a photo placeholder. I will talk you
through one or two dishes as examples. Then write it as a clean, reusable template
I can fill in for every item.
```

### Train the trainer guide
```
Help me write a train the trainer guide for [group or concept name], so our best
team members can teach new hires consistently. Cover how to run a training shift,
how to use our manuals and checklists, how to give feedback, and how to sign
someone off as ready. I will talk you through it. Ask questions if I skip
something. Then write it as a clear, practical guide.
```
